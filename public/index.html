📖 How to Play

🎯 The Goal

THE INSIDERS
Secretly eliminate the others and survive to the end.
THE INNOCENTS
Find and banish every Insider before they take over.

🔑 Setup

  • 4–30 players join with the same game code.
  • The host chooses how many Insiders (usually 2–3).
  • Insiders know each other's identities. Innocents know nothing.
  • Everyone else is an Innocent — the majority team.

🔄 Each Round

  1. Night — Insiders secretly pick a target. All Insiders must agree on the same target and lock in. Meanwhile, everyone else plays a short Night Challenge mini-game on their phone.
  2. Morning — Players check who showed up. A missing player was eliminated in the night. Their role is not revealed.
  3. Discussion — Everyone debates, accuses, and defends. Insiders must blend in and mislead.
  4. Banishment Vote — All living players vote to banish someone. Most votes loses. After a dramatic countdown, the banished player's role is revealed unless the host enabled “Hide Identity Reveals” for late game.

💰 Night Challenges & Prize Pool

  • During Night, Innocents play a mini-game to build a shared pot.
  • The host can choose the pot mode in Advanced Settings:
  • Cash — the pot increases each night based on group completion (up to a nightly target). Winners split the cash.
  • Shots — the pot increases in quarter-shot steps based on group completion (0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% of “Shots per Night”). Losers split the shots.
  • The host can also choose which Night Challenges are allowed to appear (at least one must stay enabled).

🤝 Recruitment

  • When an Insider is banished, the remaining Insiders may get a one-time chance to recruit an Innocent to their side during the next night.
  • If there is only one Insider left with 5 or more players remaining, they must recruit instead of eliminating.
  • A recruited player immediately becomes an Insider and learns who the other Insiders are.

🏁 End Game

  • The Finale triggers automatically when the remaining player count reaches the host’s Finale setting (3–6).
  • In the Finale, there are no more night eliminations.
  • After each banishment, all remaining players vote: End the game or Keep banishing.
  • If all players unanimously vote to end, the game ends immediately — Insiders win if any survive.
  • If even one player votes to keep banishing, another banishment round happens and the vote repeats.
  • Innocents win if every Insider is banished.
  • Insiders win if at least one Insider is still alive when the game ends.

💡 Tips

  • Innocents: pay attention to who pushes suspicion onto others and who deflects.
  • Insiders: act like an Innocent — vote strategically to avoid drawing suspicion.
  • Runoff votes happen if there's a tie. If the runoff is also tied, it resolves by the host’s “Persistent Tie Breaker” setting (host choice or random draw).
  • At night, keep your phone private — the game is designed so everyone has something to look at.

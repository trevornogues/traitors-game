📖 How to Play

🎯 The Goal

THE INSIDERS
Secretly eliminate the others and survive to the end.
THE INNOCENTS
Find and banish every Insider before they take over.

🔑 Setup

  • 4–30 players join with the same game code.
  • The host chooses how many Insiders (usually 2–3) and configures Advanced Settings before starting.
  • Insiders know each other's identities. Innocents know nothing.
  • Everyone else is an Innocent — the majority team.

🔄 Each Round

  1. Night — Insiders secretly pick a target. All Insiders must agree on the same target and lock in. Meanwhile, everyone else plays a short Night Challenge mini-game on their phone. If Shields are enabled, the best-scoring Innocent silently wins a shield.
  2. Morning — If Shields are enabled, a hint appears first ("a shield was / was not won") before the reveal. Then players check who showed up. A missing player was eliminated in the night. Their role is not revealed.
  3. Discussion — Everyone debates, accuses, and defends. Insiders must blend in and mislead.
  4. Banishment Vote — All living players vote to banish someone. Most votes loses. After a dramatic countdown, the banished player's role is revealed unless the host enabled “Hide Identity Reveals” for late game.

💰 Night Challenges & Prize Pool

  • During Night, Innocents play a mini-game to build a shared pot.
  • The host can choose the pot mode in Advanced Settings:
  • Cash — the pot increases each night based on group completion (up to a nightly target). Winners split the cash.
  • Shots — the pot increases in quarter-shot steps based on group completion (0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% of “Shots per Night”). Losers split the shots.
  • The host can also choose which Night Challenges are allowed to appear (at least one must stay enabled).

🛡️ Shields

  • Each night, the best-scoring Innocent on the Night Challenge secretly wins a shield — nobody is told, not even the winner.
  • If the Insiders target the shield holder, the murder is silently blocked and everyone walks in as normal.
  • Before the reveal, a hint is shown: "A shield was won" or "No shield was won" — but nothing more.
  • Shields last one night only. Ties are broken by who finished the mini-game fastest.
  • The full Shield Results are revealed at the end of the game.

⚙️ Host Settings

  • 🎭 Role Assignment — Random (default): equal odds for everyone. Weighted: while waiting in the lobby, each player privately rates how badly they want to be the bad guy on a scale of 1–5. The game uses those ratings to skew role assignments — higher ratings mean better odds — but it's still random, so nothing is guaranteed.
  • 🔥 Finale — Player Count — how many players trigger End Game mode (3–6, default 5).
  • 🎭 Hide Identity Reveals After — stops showing the banished player's role when this many or fewer players remain (3–6, default 4). Keeps the finale mysterious.
  • ⚖️ Persistent Tie Breaker — if the runoff vote is also tied: Host Chooses (host picks manually) or Random Draw (server picks instantly).
  • 🏆 Prize Pool Mode — Cash: winners split the pot. Shots: losers split the shots.
  • 🎲 Night Challenges — toggle which mini-games can appear. At least one must stay enabled.
  • 🛡️ Shields — the shield mechanic is on by default. See the Shields section above for full details.

🤝 Recruitment

  • If there is only one Insider left and enough players remain (host-configurable threshold, default 6), they must recruit instead of eliminating that night.
  • When "Recruit When Down to 2" is enabled (default on): after any banishment that leaves exactly 2+ Insiders alive, they get a one-time optional chance to recruit an Innocent that night.
  • A recruited player immediately becomes an Insider and learns who the other Insiders are.

🏁 End Game

  • The Finale triggers automatically when the remaining player count reaches the host’s Finale setting (3–6).
  • In the Finale, there are no more night eliminations.
  • After each banishment, all remaining players vote: End the game or Keep banishing.
  • If all players unanimously vote to end, the game ends immediately — Insiders win if any survive.
  • If even one player votes to keep banishing, another banishment round happens and the vote repeats.
  • Innocents win if every Insider is banished.
  • Insiders win if at least one Insider is still alive when the game ends.

💡 Tips

  • Innocents: pay attention to who pushes suspicion onto others and who deflects.
  • Insiders: act like an Innocent — vote strategically to avoid drawing suspicion.
  • Runoff votes happen if there's a tie. If the runoff is also tied, it resolves by the host's "Persistent Tie Breaker" setting (host choice or random draw).
  • At night, keep your phone private — the game is designed so everyone has something to look at.
